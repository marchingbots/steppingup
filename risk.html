Risk Management



Good Strategy

1. Direct efforts to assessment and prevention rather than damage control
2. Integrate risk management into work habits and practice processes
3. Prioritse managmenet in the practice management agenda and treat it as real work


Primary risk mgmt activity is crisis control AFTER client dissatisfaction.


Professional liability Risk

Reduce exposure by:

1. specialise or limit the services you provide;
2. hire good people;
3. provide good training;
4. understand basis of negligence claims and client dissatisfaction; and
5. professional idemnity insurance.



Benefits

1. New and repeat business - be seen as a good risk.
2. Revenue unimpactef by bad debts, discounts, WIP that clients won't pay
3. No uninsured losses
4. No reputation loss
5. Business more efficient
6. Constant improvement
7. Business continuity - minimal interruption to workflow
8. Talent retention via high professional standards


Mindset

1. Working ON the business versus IN the business
- eg. lawyer vs practice manager;

2. How much billable time allocated to risk management.

3. Managing risk mostly about managing people



Understanding RM

1. Risk is something that can hurt you
- put you out of business
- damage practice assets

2. Advance Risk topics
- risk as OCCURRNCE OF adverse event VS risk as UNCERTAINTY of event
- mananing PROBABILITY of adverse event VS managing flow-on effects of the event

3. Delay of providing services
- negatively impacts customer satisfaction, legal liability, cash flow
- SOLUTION: adequacy of resources, supervision, workflow systems
- managing effects = client communication, indemnity insurance, increasing overdraft

DO SOMETHING TODAY TO SAFEGUARD AGAINST BEING HURT TOMORROW.
