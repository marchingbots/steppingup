LPM

Legal Project Management:
The application of knowledge, skills, tools, and techniques to project activities to meet project requirements. Project management is accomplished through the application and integration of the project management processes of initiating, planning, executing, monitoring and closing.

What do LPMs do?
Legal project managers plan, organise, motivate and control resources (mainly people) to deliver the clientâ€™s specific goals. They are an enabler who removes roadblocks and ensures the required work is completed and that the right resources are applied to the work at the right time.

They manage TIME, SCOPE and COST.

LPM Framework:
1. Definition: identifying the client's requirements
2. Planning: Plan the work that needs to be done
3. Execution: Undertake the work to deliver client's requirements
4. Close out: Review and close the file.

1. Definition


Why?
why are we being asked to do the work? Define the problem needing to be solved.

What?
what is a successful outcome for the client? what are the risk? areas lacking information? budget expectations? what work will be done? what work won't be done?

Who?
who has an interest in the matter? who else needs to be involved? what will the project team look like?

When?
when do things need to happen? client has specific time requirements? any key dates or events as hard deadlines? do we need to report on progress?

Where?
where does the instruction exist? what is its context/environment? did the client agree to the approved definition document?

2. Planning


- break down deliverables to enable action plan
- allows LPM to monitor control time, costs, scope

Plan instruction before undertaking any work
- where start to finish planning not possible, use PHASED PLANNING
- use milestones, decision dates
- when decision point reached, develop plan to next decision point.
Phased planning is ROLLING WAVE PLANNING.

First step is identify work required to deliver project objectives.
- deconstruct deliverables into smaller components of work
- then configure resources, costs, time durations

3. Execution


- deliverables created and presente to the client.
- LPM manages and controls resources to ensure work is done.

Regular communication meetings:
- 3 x 3 x 3 approach:
- identifies 3 things completed since previous reporting period, 3 things planned for completion in next reporting period, 3 issues being wrangled with

LPM exercises CHANGE CONTROL
- ensures all requested changes and agreed actions are recorded and accountd for
- uncontrolled changes referred to as SCOPE CREEP = one of the main causes of project failure

Partnership QUestions

1. What is the equity structure of the partnership?
2. What are the capital contributions required of partners?
If there is a capital contribution, on what basis is it to be repaid?
3. what is the remuneration/profit distribution model?
4. On what basis does or can a partner increase their profit share, or advance to equity?
5. what is the governance structure of the firm?
6. On what basis may other partners terminate your partnership?
7. What are the notification requirements for termination?
8. what voting rights do partners have?
9. what are admission process for new partners?
10. if fees are dominant criterion for determining profit, how will I fare?

Company Finances Assessment

1. Check firm's accounts for past 3 years including current budget and financial report
2. Look at firm's assets and liabilities.
3. What are current account balances? Are there funds to cover debts/distribute balances to owners?
4. Look at income with statements of drawings
5. are there payments to owners which constitute perks? do they benefit all owners equally?
6. Does the firm produce regular cash flow statements?
7. How are owners paid? Based on monthly profit? regular periods?
