NZ - Company
- under LCA, must be incorporated law firm
- only lawyers invovled in provision of lawery's regulated services may be directors of the company or hold shares carrying voter rights
family members or related trust may hold shares with no voting rights
no joint or several liability except where funds entrusted to lawyer/firm are lost by way of theft.

Indo - Partnership
- read partnership law act nz
- every partner is agent of the firm and every other partner in the firm
- actions of 1 partner binds the firm and other partners
- every partner jointly and severally liable with other partners for all debts and obligations of the firm while they are a partner. Carries over into deceased partner's estate if any obligation remains unsatisfied
- every partner jointly & severally liable for wrongful or negligent act/ommission of any other partner


Partnership QUestions

1. What is the equity structure of the partnership?
2. What are the capital contributions required of partners?
If there is a capital contribution, on what basis is it to be repaid?
3. what is the remuneration/profit distribution model?
4. On what basis does or can a partner increase their profit share, or advance to equity?
5. what is the governance structure of the firm?
6. On what basis may other partners terminate your partnership?
7. What are the notification requirements for termination?
8. what voting rights do partners have?
9. what are admission process for new partners?
10. if fees are dominant criterion for determining profit, how will I fare?

Company Finances Assessment

1. Check firm's accounts for past 3 years including current budget and financial report
2. Look at firm's assets and liabilities.
3. What are current account balances? Are there funds to cover debts/distribute balances to owners?
4. Look at income with statements of drawings
5. are there payments to owners which constitute perks? do they benefit all owners equally?
6. Does the firm produce regular cash flow statements?
7. How are owners paid? Based on monthly profit? regular periods?

• Admission of new partners/shareholders;
• What are the arrangements for the payment of capital or other funding?
• What about the payment of goodwill?
• What are the profit-sharing provisions? Do they allow for equal sharing, lockstep, or some basis on which each partner/shareholder is remunerated depending largely on the fees they generate?
• If fees are a dominant criterion in determining profit, how will you fare?
• If there are owners that are also in a governance or management role, what is their tenure and what powers do they have? How will that work affect the fees they generate? Is such work a duplication of the office manager or other such employee, and how are the conflicts between governance, management and operations dealt with (if that owner does all three)?
• What are the powers of any practice manager or other non-owner manager?
• Will you be required to give management a power of attorney to sign documents on your behalf?
• Are there provisions on the holding of regular meetings? What are the rules for voting at such meetings?
• Will an owner who is sick or partially or permanently disabled be entitled to continue to draw profits for a time, and does the firm provide insurance to cover ongoing disablement? If there is no cover, are the owners required to have their own cover? If not, it may be desirable for you to take out your own insurance to protect you against loss of income;
• What holidays are owners entitled to? What about parental leave?
• Is there a sabbatical scheme or an entitlement to long-service leave?
• On what basis are owners reimbursed for expenses?
• What are the retirement and succession provisions? Is there an age at which you are expected to retire?
• Is there a restraint of trade preventing an owner from leaving and competing against it?
Is it enforceable?
• Are there provisions covering expulsion of owners from the firm?
• Are there bottom lines or minimum standards of performance or behaviour which have to be observed in order to remain an owner?
